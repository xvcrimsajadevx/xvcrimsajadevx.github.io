Tol Kyn

Race: Human/Desert Nomads/Lost Tribe

Country/Region: Teran A'ao

Lifespan: 150-175 years

Appearance:

Tall, with dark or tan skin tones. Males are born with identifiable birth marks on their hands and wrists, females with markings on their necks and arms. Males and Females ware the traditional leather garb/armor of their tribe. A tattoo of their tribal name is etched above their eye soon after birth.

Personality Traits:

To those who have seen Tol Kyn in battle, they are determined, methodical, and without fear. Tol Kyn soldiers were highly prized mercenaries in their early history. They are rarely caught off guard, and have little respect for most other tribes and peoples. Their leaders have varied from ruthless to honorable and wise, and depending on the era, they can be saviors or villains.

History:

While little is known about them, The Tol Kyn are thought to be descended from the first Human inhabitants of Teran A'ao. They did not at first have a written languauge and so their precise origin remains a mystery. One legend of the Tol Kyn speaks of their first chieftain marrying a Magi princess, and carving a small kingdom from the desert. It is believed that the Tol Kyn (Sons of the First) were once a proud respected people numbering in the tens of thousands, but after an unknown calamity and a deadly scourge of unknown origin, their numbers diminished until the present day. Their sister tribe the Tal' Kyre (Daughters of the Second) were almost destroyed by famine and plague, and their knowledge of hand to hand combat was combined with the warrior heritage of the Tol Kyn. Females and males are encouraged to become warriors, and at the age 18 are sent to make their way to the pilgrimage spot of the ancestors...a stone shrine near the coast.

The spear is the singular and most recognizable weapon of the Tol Kyn. Typically 6 feet in length with leather grips and an iron or copper tip. The spear is the outward visible sign of a warrior, as are the protective coverings on the hands known as Cestus bands. They are taught that the spear is their life, and their death. Upon the end of a warriors life, the spear is burned and the tip forged into a new spear, and given to the eldest son or daughter.

Tol Kyn females were often considered very amorous, and driven by their desires. It was not uncommon for one Tol Kyn woman to have two or three husbands, provided that she respect all equally. Several of the more ruthless Tol Kyn leaders have been women, one in particular Shir' Tal Kyn. Shir ruthlessly slaughtered trespassers on Tol Kyn land and strived to wipe out all families along with male chiefs not loyal to her. Shir was defeated by Tyna Tol Kyn, but not before she vanished in a sandstorm with her entire army never to be seen again. Legend speaks of a time when Shir' would return, and this story is told to young Tol Kyn as a nursery rhyme.


::Fear my Kyn, Fear her name,
for no one knows from whence she came

At night when tombs of fear surprise
till judgment day her corpse arise
Shir will come calling near
and feel your hearts with mortal fear

She comes with lance and spear and bow
and all the land around shall know
your death is nigh' and heart shall fail
and not one man shall tell the tale.::


Family Namesa:

Tol Kyn refers to the males of the tribe and the tribal name itself. While there are several families within the Tol that claim this name, a chieftan can rise from any family. Tol is typically the surname of males with Tal being the surname of males. R'yas Tol Kyn (male), Ul'as Tal Krei (Female). There are at least 12 different family names typically starting with a 'K' sounding consonant.

Magic:

Tol Kyn males are usually not imbued with any special abilities, Tol Kyn females however are frequently trained in the magical arts, and usually have some ability with elemental forces. Most notably fire and wind. At a young age, female Tol Kyn are normally separated from the tribe and sent to train with the tribal high priestess. Tol Kyn revere all magical entities, but are not taken to worship any except on rare occasions of need.

Extra Info:

Very few if any Tol Kyn remain in the known world, though survivors of this tribe are claimed in parts unknown, there is little to know evidence of their survival.
