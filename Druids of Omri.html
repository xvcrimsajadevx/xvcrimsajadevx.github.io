Druids of Omri



RACE: Human (magic users, untrained)

COUNTRY/REGION: All over the Nether Realm, they congregate around Omri's temples, which are situated in the forests of Avalon, the Eastern Continent, and even some in Lenal...

LIFESPAN: As magic users they have an extended lifespan that matched wizards and sorcerers, but as they are untrained and do not know how to stop their appearance from looking older, they age normally until they are old, but are not frail. When druids get to an extremely old age, they 'return to the earth'. Some believe they are reincarnated as trees or animals, only Omri knows the truth.

APPEARANCE:

Typical Druidic apparel, robes and hoods that tend to hide their faces. They spend most of their time in forests and temples dedicated to their Djinn, they go barefooted mostly, and wear nothing under their robes which are tied at the waist with a rope belt. They tend to grow their hair long, men and women, and the men grow their beards. There are some druids clans in which they raise their children, but most of the temple clans are child free and consisted of adults who have gone on a pilgrimage to find Omri. These individuals come from varied backgrounds. Some will be brought up Druids, others will have discovered their powers to use magic and chose this strange religion as a way to channel their powers. Converts and several generations of Druids live together in harmony, serving Omri their only desire, creating a peaceful community.

PERSONALITY TRAITS:

Almost monastic in their temper, they are very calm and very much a spiritual people. They believe in keeping the Great Balance, and using their magic to do this. A religious people who adhere to their Druidic rituals on a daily basis, they are extremely philosophical and quite open minded.

HISTORY:

The origins of the druids of Omri are debatable. Most believe that Omri himself drew a select few humans to him and taught them the ways of Nature. He taught them how to control the element of earth, how to speak to the tree spirits, work with the dryads to preserve the forests, and heal human flesh and that of plants and animals. Over the generations their link with Omri became stronger and those who dedicated their life to him acquired unusual powers. These were not powers they were born with, most outsiders believe they are taught magics only known by their god and that he blesses them with his knowledge.

The Druids are only just starting to wane, their numbers dwindle. Their are only a very family clans belonging to the druids now and the temples are sparsely populated. They took refuge in the forests of Fenlar, but recently have lost many numbers to the bandits that have taken over the land. Omri has summoned his people to him, to his main temple on an island only druids know how to get to.

MAGIC:

The Druids of Omri are humans untrained as Wizards or Sorcerers, and have their roots deep in nature magics. Their powers are similar to the races of Titan, the difference being they use the Ether to control this element. The danger here being the risk of becoming a Earth Magi. They are taught specific spells by their 'god' Omri, and use their magic to help and to worship their 'god', the Djinn of Nature

EXTRA INFO:

The Earth Magi mainly come from this group of humans. As they are the most proficient in earth magics, apart from the few wizards and sorcerers who specialize in geo-mancy, there is a higher risk of a druid losing control and being transformed into Magi. Those druids who are unlucky enough to have this fate are luckier then most wizards or sorcerers. Omri takes them in and teaches them. They become his most powerful followers, but because of the nature of earth and of Omri himself, they remain peaceful and in balance with the world, never using their powers and status as Magi for evildoing.
