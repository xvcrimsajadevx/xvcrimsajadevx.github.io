How Noble Houses are organised


In Avalon hume noble houses rise and fall according to wealth, war and politics. There are, for the most part, always one ruling house, four high bloods, and three lower bloods. Even if these eight positions are taken that does not mean one cannot make a noble house, there is nobility below the eight main houses and these are referred to as the Base Houses. In some instances these base houses can be more prominent (more wealthy, larger, more land) then the main houses but because of democracy, a change in ideals, politics, wealth or war they can find themselves on the outside of the main houses (these eight main houses tend to make up any government in whichever domain, the heads of houses forming a senate or council that the ruler oversees. This is by no means universal and completely depends on the type of government that rules. See the geopolitical infrastructure in the Regions section for each country to see the specific type of government and their individual hierarchies). If a member wishes to apply for a higher or lower nobles house this can be negotiated if there are no places available in the country you are applying for.

    • For the noble house template please go to the Subcultures Application Template article.

    • The exception to this system (4 high bloods and 3 low bloods) is Nyoryn as it has a democracy and has elections. It does have aristocracy still so there are a set amount of Baron's ruling the Baronies. they have only one high blood house and three lower blood houses per Barony though.

    • Remember, if you add to Avalon's History you must keep an eye on other members new house applications as it may contradict your own application and we don't always notice this.

The Main Houses change according to the whims of the society, which for Adenia has varied in history, yet they have proved overall to be rather steady, with little change in the main eight houses.

FENLAR NOBLE HOUSES


The noble houses in Fenlar rise and fall according to wealth, politics and war. The Ruling House had always been House Melnoth, and Melnoth heirs always marry a House of Gallian, House Melnoth being an offshoot of House Ayrie. The eight houses are spread all over Fenlar and for the most part rule and govern their own regions. Melnoth has always resided in Deliniad and depending on the status of the cities and main towns in Fenlar, the other seven houses will rise or fall with their regions.

TERAN A'AO NOBLE HOUSES


The noble houses in Teran A'ao rise and fall according to wealth, politics and war. The Ruling House had always been House Ashari, and Ashari heirs tend to marry into the lower blood house of Tsar. Teran's houses are fairly constant, their ruling Heads being part of the Sheiks Council and his closest advisor's. Due to the nature of the land most of the eight upper houses reside close to the Sheik in Alaanzyr.

ERAOSIA NOBLE HOUSES


The noble houses in Eraosia rise and fall according to wealth, politics and war. The Ruling House has been Dra for many generations. Eraosia has the most fluid house system and new houses are always coming to dominance, in fighting and intrigue, murder and war plaguing the seven houses below Dra. All adhere to the Emperor though and he cares not what petty squabbles occur beneath his ruling house.

SEDANA NOBLE HOUSES


Sedana's ruling House depends wholly upon the Sovereign and the Sovereign is chosen by the Priests of Zadok and the Watchers. Noble Houses in Sedana are fairly consistent, the country being mostly peaceful, and the lower houses are considered the middle class, as well as many of the Base Houses. Anyone can make their fortune and rise to a place in the nobility, but the higher nobles and some base houses are the ones who are steady and have been around for many years. It is not uncommon for a newly made sovereign to have a house created due to his ascension. When he leaves office his house is relegated to a Base House, retaining the wealth and honour of having had a sovereign.

NYORYN NOBLE HOUSES


Nyoryn's Baronies and their high and low bloods are fairly stalwart. It is the base houses that have much fluidity and self made wealth such as that of merchants are often elevated to this position. As Nyoryn has citizens from all over the world coming to them this means that anyone in the world can make their name and earn nobility in the free land of Nyoryn. There is also the opportunity to be voted in as Magnate, thus giving your family the status of nobility (as a base house) via politics.

In the distant past before the Nexus destroyed Gallian many noble houses intermarried with that of Fenlar, so their bloodlines are often preserved as base houses in that Kingdom.
